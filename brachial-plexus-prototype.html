Common Brachial Plexopathies

Erb's Palsy (Upper Trunk)
Most common traumatic plexopathy. Results from head-shoulder traction injuries affecting C5-C6 roots.
Klumpke's Palsy (Lower Trunk)
Severe hand weakness from C8-T1 fiber damage. Often from arm-pulling traction injuries.
Neuralgic Amyotrophy
Parsonage-Turner syndrome. Immune-mediated plexitis with severe pain followed by weakness.
Postoperative
Common after cardiac surgery. Usually affects lower trunk/medial cord from stretch injury.
Radiation Injury
Delayed progressive plexopathy. Myokymic discharges are pathognomonic on EMG.
Thoracic Outlet Syndrome
True neurogenic TOS from fibrous band. Preferentially affects T1 fibers and thenar muscles.
